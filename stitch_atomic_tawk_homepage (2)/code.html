Section I

Initial Condition Assessment

The subject power plant, a mid-century overhead valve configuration, was extracted from the heavy-duty sector. Initial visual logs indicate severe carbonization of the intake tracts and extensive galvanic corrosion across the fastener heads.

DANGER: Residual thermal energy and chemical stability are unconfirmed. Technicians must maintain a 3-meter safety perimeter until the core temperature stabilizes. Do not engage the crank assembly without high-viscosity lubrication flood.

II. Disassembly Protocol

Technicians must utilize Grade-A chrome vanadium tooling. Impact drivers are strictly prohibited on the cylinder head bolts to prevent block fracture.

Apply 44-B Solvent and allow 12 hours for penetration before attempting initial torque break.
